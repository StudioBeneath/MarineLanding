Wills and Estates

Under the provincial Wills, Estates and Succession Act (WESA), a will is a legal document that provides instructions which one must follow upon a person’s death in regards to the handling of their estate.

We prepare and execute a will tailored to your needs and help you define the executor of your will, the guardian for your children, and the trustee of your estate. All these important decisions should be made carefully and in complete trust.

Who Should Have a Will?

Adults in BC who:

  • Own property, including real estate, vehicles, and/or other assets;
  • Have a dependent spouse or children;
  • Wish to have someone they know and trust take care of their estate after their death.

We will prepare a will that fits your needs at a reasonable cost. If you own assets in British Columbia as well as around the world, it is possible to have a will that relates only with your B.C. assets. Without a will in B.C. upon your death, your estate will be administered pursuant to the Wills, Estates and Succession Act (WESA) by the Supreme Court rather than by choices and decisions made by you.

Real Estate Purchase and Sale

Purchasing and selling your home is one of the biggest decisions and financial investments of your life. We are here to help you through the process — also known as conveyance — from when property is conveyed from the seller (vendor) to the buyer (purchaser). We will support you with the necessary documents to make the transition to/from home ownership as smooth as possible.

How it works

Land ownership in B.C. is recorded in the Land Title Office. As notaries, we assist you with the following:

  • Form A Transfer: a document for the current owner to sign and confirm the agreement of transfer ownership and agreed purchase price.
  • Property Transfer Tax: a form required under B.C. law for every property transfer transaction.
  • Form F: a certificate of payment for condos and strata property to confirm strata fees and assessments have been paid.
  • Buyer’s and Seller’s Statement of Adjustments: any items such as strata fees or property taxes which are adjusted between the buyer and seller, dependant on who paid the expenses and when sale is completed.
  • State of Title: a document for the Registrar of Land Titles to guarantee that the individual named as the “Registered Owners” is in fact the listed owner.

Powers of Attorney

Also known as POA, a Power of Attorney is a document that allows an individual to authorize another person (attorney) to act on their behalf.

The POA gives the representative authority to make decisions when unable to act for themselves. Usually, a POA exists as a person for financial arrangements, such as buying and selling property and signing tax returns. A POA can be arranged for a specific purpose during a specific period of time or be arranged for more general purposes.

POA can be dismissed or changed at any time.

Personal Planning Documents

In B.C., the Representation Agreement is an important document which specifies who has the right to make decisions in relation to healthcare on your behalf.

This personal planning document includes refusal of life support and healthcare decisions that are important to the individual. The agreement is valuable for individuals with no partner or children, or if their children are in conflict with one another about a decision involving the individual’s health.

We will work together with families to define a representative they see fit to act on behalf of the individual.

A Representation Agreement can include decisions involving but not limited to:

  • Routine finances
  • Health and personal care
  • Refusal or consent to life-support treatment and care
  • Consent to medical procedures
  • Living arrangements

Mortgage Refinancing

A mortgage refinancing, or home equity loan, gives an individual the opportunity to leverage the value of their home to access funds for renovations, tuition, investments, or other life decisions.

Most homeowners have significant equity to their homes, either by paying off a mortgage or increase of home value in the market. Mortgage refinancing includes borrowing money against the current value of their home.

We will help navigate the refinancing process by providing legal advice, preparing necessary documents, and assisting in making funds available to meet the needs of those life decisions.

Notarization

When we notarize a document, we are administering an oath, affirmation, or declaration over the document(s).

We notarize a variety of personal documents, including but not limited to:

  • Statutory Declarations
  • Insurance Loss Declarations
  • Certified True Copies of Documents
  • Notarizations and Attestations of Signatures
  • Execution or Authentications of International Documents
  • Letters of Invitation for Foreign Travel
  • Authorizations of Minor Child Travel
  • Proof of Identity for Travel Purposes
  • Passport Application Documentation
  • Identity Documents Required For Your Notarization Appointment
